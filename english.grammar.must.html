Final Exam: Modals of Obligation & Advice

Teacher's Note: This test evaluates should/shouldn't (suggestions), must/mustn't (rules/prohibitions), and have to/don't have to (necessity).
Estudiante: Lee con atención el contexto de cada frase. ¡Buena suerte!

Section 1: Fill in the Blanks (10 points)

Complete with: should, shouldn't, must, mustn't, have to, don't have to.

1. You talk when the teacher is explaining the lesson. It's rude!
2. It's raining outside. I think you take your raincoat.
3. In the UK, people drive on the left side of the road. It's the law.
4. You eat so many sweets; they are bad for your teeth.
5. My mother says I clean my room before I go out with my friends.
6. We go to school tomorrow because it is a local holiday in Madrid.
7. Shhh! You be quiet in the library. People are studying.
8. If you feel sick, you stay in bed and rest.
9. Look at that sign: "No Dogs Allowed". You bring your dog inside.
10. Do I finish this homework today? "No, you can do it tomorrow."

Section 2: Multiple Choice (10 points)

Choose the best option (a, b, or c) for each situation.

a) must    b) have to    c) should

20. We _____ buy more milk. There are three bottles in the fridge.
a) mustn't    b) shouldn't    c) don't have to

Section 3: Sentence Transformation (10 points)

Rewrite or complete the sentences using the word in brackets.
